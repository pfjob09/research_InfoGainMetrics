Abstract—This paper addresses the problem of
monotone scattered data interpolation. An interpolation
scheme is developed to interpolate the scattered data
assembled over the triangular grid. The developed
interpolation scheme utilizes a family of C 1 rational
cubic function. The edges of each triangle are
interpolated by the C 1 rational cubic function. The C 1
rational cubic function is also used to join vertices to
their opposite edges. This practice provide three vertex
boundary interpolating functions. Resultant rational
triangular function is the convex combination of these
vertex boundary interpolants. The developed rational
interpolating scheme assures C 1 continuity at each
vertex of triangle and along the boundaries of each
triangle. Since the rational function has parameters in
its definition, so these are inherited in rational
triangular function. The constraints are developed on
half of these parameters to preserve the shape of data
while remaining are free for shape modification.

An interpolating scheme that preserves the shape of
data is a valuable tool for data visualization. Physical
phenomenon give rise to data which can be positive,
monotone or convex or a combination of these shapes
characteristics. Monotony often arises in data from a
variety of sources e.g. pharmacological data of doseresponse, pricing model, assessment of physical and
chemical potentials and data arising in multi criterion
decision making and fuzzy logic.
The fitting of monotony preserving function to
discrete scattered data remained a subject of interest
in last few decades. Some of the noticeable
contributions can be found in [1,3, 5,7]. Beatson and
Ziegler [1] interpolated monotone regular data by C 1
monotone quadratic spline. The authors, in [1],
exploited functional and derivative values to ensure
monotony. Thus the developed scheme in [1] was not
applicable to data with derivatives. Delgado and Peña
[3] investigated the monotony of rectangular and
triangular rational Bézier surfaces and proved that
those were not ‘axially monotony preserving.’ Floater
and Peña [5] brought forth three categories of
monotone functions on triangles. To preserve the
shape of monotone scattered data, Han and
Schumaker [7] scheme was based on the

The motivation of this paper is the research work
presented in [8] and [9]. In [8] and [9] rational,
functions with parameters were used for shape
preservation of regular surface data. In this paper, a
family of rational cubic functions with parameters is
used to interpolate the boundaries of each triangle as
well as its vertex boundary interpolation. The
approach results in a rational triangular interpolating
function with 24 parameters in each triangular patch.
Half of the parameters have been constrained to
preserve the shape of monotone scattered data while
the remaining ones were free parameters to modify
the shape of surface at user’s choice.
